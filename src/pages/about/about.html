About SoCal Cigar Shops

This is a personal project originally developed for the Front End JavaScript course that I took while attending Udacity's Full Stack Web Development Nano Degree program. It was my expression of the "Neighborhood Map" project in that course.

It attempts to answer the question "Where are the good cigar stores in Southern California". It aggregates the tools that I would use (Google & Yelp) to answer this question. I'm attempting to improve on both of those tools by actively ignoring those establishments that a real cigar smoker wouldn't consider to be a real cigar store.

What is a Real Cigar Store?

Cigar smoking is a social activity. There is a camaraderie of cigar smokers that real cigar stores facilitate.

So by my humble definition a real cigar store is one where cigar smokers can both buy cigars and also sit with others and smoke them. Thankfully this isn't all that hard to find in Southern California.

A real cigar store has several characteristics:

  • An establishment whose main business is selling cigars and tobacco pipes - in short - a tobacconist
  • A walk-in humidor with a wide range of cigars.
  • A sales person who actually smokes cigars and is knowledgable about them.
  • A sales person who can carry on a conversation about the cigars in English.
  • A relaxing place where the cigars purchased there can be smoked.
  • Very little to none of the non-tobacco paraphernalia found in head shops and cheap cigarette stores. Paraphernalia such as "glass", vaporizers, vape juice and cigarettes by the case are not characteristic of a real cigar store.
  • Friendly patrons are always a plus.

Sure, you can buy cigars at convenience stores and large liquor stores. The experience however, is just as impersonal as buying cigars online. There is a time and place for it but in those situations you are participating in a simple transaction. When you buy a cigar at a real cigar store you promote the ethic and camaraderie of the real cigar smoker.

Thank God for California

California is the only state on the west coast where one can buy a cigar and smoke it the same establishment.

In Washington and Oregon the only exception to this is in Indian owned casinos. Washington does make some provision for outdoor smoking areas adjacent to cigar stores but the weather there is rarely conducive.

Every time I light up I think a little thanks for California.
